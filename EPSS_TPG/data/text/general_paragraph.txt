Cloud computing has revolutionized the software industry over the past decade. Companies like Amazon, Microsoft, and Google now provide scalable infrastructure to millions of developers worldwide.

However, migrating legacy systems to the cloud introduces significant challenges. Database configurations must be carefully reviewed, and network security policies need to be updated. Many organizations have experienced downtime during migration because their teams underestimated the complexity of the process.

In response, DevOps engineers have adopted containerization tools such as Docker and Kubernetes. These tools allow teams to package applications with their dependencies, ensuring consistent behavior across development, staging, and production environments. Consequently, deployment failures have decreased by nearly forty percent in organizations that embraced this approach.
